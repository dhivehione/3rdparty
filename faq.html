What Is This?

What is the 3d Party?
A digital ladder to the corridors of power — no bribe, no family name, no "who's your uncle?" required. We're a direct democracy platform where you vote on laws and policies yourself. No middlemen. No gatekeepers.

Think of it as a digital town hall that never closes. And doesn't care about your last name.
Are you an insurance company?
We get this a lot. No — we're a political movement. The "3d" stands for "Third Option" — the alternative to the two parties everyone already knows are broken.

We considered "3rd Option Party" but someone pointed out that still sounds like insurance. So we kept the name and the joke. At least you'll remember us.
Who started this?
A group of Maldivian citizens who got tired of the same two options every election. The founders are normal people — not politicians, not wealthy tycoons, just people who thought "there has to be a better way."

The whitepaper has the full backstory. Or you can join and ask us directly.

How Does It Work?

What is Merit Score?
Your Merit Score is your influence in the movement. Not based on money, not based on who you know — based on what you actually contribute.

You earn points by:
• Voting on proposals
• Writing successful policies
• Bringing in active new members
• Completing tasks on the bounty board (legal research, design, etc.)
• Donations (with steep diminishing returns — $10 gets more influence proportionally than $10,000)

Old contributions decay over time (18-month half-life) so you have to keep participating. Sitting on early membership doesn't grant eternal power.
How do I actually vote on laws?
Once you're a member, you can go to our Laws page and vote on individual clauses, articles, or entire laws. Yes, down to the specific clause level.

You can support, oppose, or abstain. You can also write why you voted that way. Everything is public.

Vote weight? Right now it's one-person-one-vote. Later, when we hit enough members, it becomes merit-weighted. But even then, your vote still counts.
Can I propose my own laws?
Yes. Any verified member can submit a policy proposal.

Process:
1. Write your proposal (must be at least 20 characters, no spam)
2. Stake a small portion of your Merit Score (shows you're serious)
3. Wait for 8-hour cooldown (prevents proposal spam)
4. It goes to a public referendum
5. Members vote. If it passes, it becomes part of our platform agenda.

If it fails with less than 10% support, you lose some points (reputation penalty). If it passes, great — you just shaped policy.
What if no one agrees with my proposal?
That's democracy — not every idea wins.

But there's a "Bridge-Building" bonus: if you amend a failing proposal and it passes, you get a massive 400-point Merit Bonus. So if your idea is almost good but not quite, fix it instead of giving up.

The system rewards compromise and unity, not just having the loudest opinion.

Joining & Membership

How do I join?
Go to the Join page. Enter your phone (7 digits) and NID number. That's it. Takes about 45 seconds.

No email required. No complicated forms. No "donation to join" requirement.
Is my data safe?
We store your phone and NID for verification only. No data is sold, shared, or sent to anyone.

How you vote is private (only you see your vote). But the results are public, so everyone can see what the community decided.

The code will be open-source so anyone can verify we're not doing anything sketchy. Transparency isn't optional for us — it's the whole point.
Can I leave anytime?
You can stop participating at any time. But if you leave and come back, your Merit Score resets to zero.

This is intentional. It encourages people to work through disagreements instead of walking away when things get tough. Movements that fall apart when people leave aren't movements — they're audiences.

Leadership & Power

Are there leaders?
You're a leader the moment you join. This isn't a movement with a handful of chiefs and thousands of followers. Every member is expected to contribute, vote, propose, build.

When we reach 1,000 members, we'll form a Governing Council elected from within the movement — think project managers with term limits. They can't make major decisions alone; everything significant requires a member vote. They're elected, serve 2-year terms, and can be recalled if they underperform.

Until then, the movement runs on direct member participation. Everyone has a path to leadership — the first step is simply joining.

In old parties, you join to follow. Here, you join to lead. The only question is: where do you want to take us?
How do I become a leader?
There are two paths:

Internal leadership (Governing Council):
1. We need 1,000 members first
2. You need minimum 500 Merit Score

Once both are met: apply, do a live public interview (yes, on video), then get voted in by members via ranked-choice voting.

National leadership (when we form government):
Any member can apply for any high government post — Cabinet Minister, Ambassador, Independent Commission President, SOE Director, Government SEO Chairperson. The selection happens live on national TV — interviews judged by top experts, with public voting. The President cannot appoint family or friends. Every appointee gets a 3-month probation and annual appraisals.

Both paths are merit-based, not familiarity-based. We don't do "familiar face" elections.
What if leaders misbehave?
When we have leadership (Governing Council or national government appointees), accountability is built in:
• Yearly appraisals — reviewed annually, just like a real job
• Term limits — max 2 years, then must be re-elected
• Recall vote — if 10% of members sign a petition, we vote on removal. 80% says go = they're gone
• Decision statements — all decisions are documented and public

The whitepaper calls this "Limits on Leadership Authority." We actually mean it. No exceptions.

Legality & Power

Is this even legal in the Maldives?
Yes. We're not doing anything illegal — we're a group of citizens discussing and voting on policies.

Right now, we function as an "advocacy and public-will barometer" — we can show what the people actually want, even without formal legal status.

If we gain political influence and win elections, proposals that passed our referendum process will become the actual policies we implement. That's the plan.
What happens when you win elections?
Our elected candidates are bound to the "People's Manifesto" — the set of high-passing policies that members already voted for.

If an elected representative consistently votes against the platform's referendums, we can initiate a recall and field a challenger against them in the next election.

It's a digital leash — they're delegates, not rulers.
What about coalition government?
If we need to form a coalition, there's a protocol (activated when we have a Governing Council):
1. Governing Council proposes a "Negotiation Mandate" to members
2. Members vote on it (supermajority required)
3. If approved, we negotiate publicly (regular updates on the platform)
4. Final deal goes to members for final ratification

Members decide, not the leadership. No backroom deals.

Money & Donations

Where does the money come from?
Member donations. That's it.

We have a "Live Treasury" — all donations are public. You can see exactly how much came in and where it went. No dark money, no mysterious donors.

The donation formula has steep diminishing returns. A $10 donation gets decent Merit points. A $10,000 donation gets proportionally far less. Wealthy people can't buy the movement.
Can corporations donate?
Yes, but it won't get them much influence. The logarithmic formula means large donations have heavily diminished returns.

A corporation donating $10,000 gets roughly the same Merit influence as someone donating $50. We think that's fair.

Plus, all donations are public. If a company donates and then gets favorable policies, everyone can see the correlation.

Still Skeptical? Good.

You should be. Every political movement promises change. Most deliver nothing. Here's how we're different — and how you can verify it yourself:

  • ✓ Open-source code — our platform will be public. Anyone can audit it.
  • ✓ Live Treasury — every donation, every expense, visible to all members.
  • ✓ Public votes — all policy results are transparent. No hidden decisions.
  • ✓ No founders' privilege — the people who started this have no special power. Merit Score applies to everyone equally.

Don't take our word for it. Look at the code. Check the treasury. Read the votes. Then decide.
