Frequently Asked Questions

Questions we get asked. Answers we actually gave. No legalese, no corporate speak.

What Even Is This?

What is the 3d Party?
We're a new political movement in the Maldives. Not a party in the traditional sense — more like a direct democracy app where you actually get to vote on laws and policies yourself. No middlemen. No "representatives" who forget what you wanted after election day.

Think of it as a digital town hall that never closes.
Are you an insurance company?
No. Stop asking. We are a political movement. The "3d" stands for "Third Option" — like, the alternative to the two parties everyone already knows are broken.

(We know the name causes confusion. We considered "3rd Option Party" but someone pointed out that still sounds like insurance. So we said screw it, keep the joke.)
Who started this?
A bunch of Maldivian citizens who got tired of the same two options every election. The founders are normal people — not politicians, not wealthy tycoons, just people who thought "there has to be a better way."

The whitepaper has details if you want the full backstory. Or you can just join and find out yourself.

How Does This Actually Work?

What is Merit Score?
Your "Merit Score" is your influence in the movement. Not based on money, not based on who you know — based on what you actually contribute.

You earn points by:
• Voting on proposals (pass = more points)
• Writing successful policies
• Bringing in active new members
• Doing tasks on the "bounty board" (legal research, design, etc.)
• Donations (but with diminishing returns — $10 gets more influence than $10,000 proportionally)

Old contributions decay over time (18-month half-life) so you gotta keep participating. Sitting on your early membership doesn't grant eternal power. Sorry early adopters, you actually have to do stuff.
How do I actually vote on laws?
Once you're a member, you can go to our Laws page and vote on individual clauses, articles, or entire laws. Yes, down to the specific clause level.

You can support, oppose, or abstain. You can also write why you voted that way. Everything is public.

Vote weight? Right now it's one-person-one-vote. Later, when we hit enough members, it becomes merit-weighted. But even then, your vote still counts.
Can I propose my own laws?
Yes! Any verified member can submit a policy proposal.

Process:
1. Write your proposal (must be at least 20 characters, no spam)
2. Stake a small portion of your Merit Score (shows you're serious)
3. Wait for 8-hour cooldown (prevents proposal spam)
4. It goes to a public referendum
5. Members vote. If it passes, it becomes part of our platform agenda.

If it fails with less than 10% support, you lose some points (reputation penalty). If it passes, great!
What if no one agrees with my proposal?
That's democracy! Not every idea wins.

BUT there's a "Bridge-Building" bonus — if you amend a failing proposal and it passes, you get a massive 400-point Merit Bonus. So if your idea is almost good but not quite, fix it instead of giving up.

The system rewards compromise and unity, not just having the loudest opinion.

Joining & Membership

How do I join?
Go to the Join page. Enter your phone (7 digits) and NID number. That's it. Takes about 45 seconds.

No email required. No complicated forms. No "donation to join" nonsense.
Is my data safe?
We store your phone and NID for verification. That's it. No data is sold, shared, or sent to anyone.

How you vote is private (only you see your vote). But the results are public, so everyone can see what the community decided.

The code will be open-source eventually so people can verify we're not doing sketchy stuff.
Can I leave anytime?
Yes, you can just stop participating. But if you leave and come back, your Merit Score resets to zero.

This is intentional. It incentivizes people to work through disagreements instead of just quitting when things get tough.

(Yes, this is basically a "don't abandon the ship" feature. We learned from other movements that fell apart when people left.)

Who's In Charge?

Are there leaders?
Yes, but they're not "in charge" in the traditional sense. They're more like project managers.

We have "Shadow Ministers" (Governing Council) who handle day-to-day operations. But they can't make major decisions alone — everything significant requires a member vote.

They're elected, have 2-year term limits, and can be recalled if they suck. Check our Leadership page for the full details.
How do I become a leader?
Two things need to happen first:
1. We need 1,000 members (leadership applications don't make sense with 10 people)
2. You need enough Merit Score (minimum 500, but positions have higher requirements)

Once both are met: apply, do a live public interview (yes, on video), then get voted in by members via ranked-choice voting.

It's actually harder than getting elected in traditional politics. We don't do "familiar face" elections.
What if leaders misbehave?
Several ways:
• Yearly appraisals — all leaders get reviewed annually
• Term limits — max 2 years, then re-elected like everyone else
• Recall vote — if 10% of members sign a petition, we vote on removing them. 80% says go = they're gone
• Decision statements — all their decisions are documented and public

The whitepaper calls this "Limits on Leadership Authority." We actually mean it.

The "Wait, Is This Legal?" Section

Is this even legal in the Maldives?
Currently, yes. We're not doing anything illegal — we're just a group of citizens discussing and voting on policies.

Right now, we function as an "advocacy and public-will barometer" — we can show what the people actually want, even without formal legal status.

If/when we gain political influence and win elections, proposals that passed our referendum process will become the actual policies we implement. That's the plan anyway.
What happens when you win elections?
Then we actually do what we said we'd do. Our candidates are bound to the "People's Manifesto" — the set of high-passing policies that members already voted for.

If an elected representative consistently votes against the platform's referendums, we can initiate a recall and field a challenger against them in the next election.

It's a "digital leash" — they're delegates, not rulers.
What about coalition government?
If we need to form a coalition, there's a whole protocol for it:
1. Governing Council proposes a "Negotiation Mandate" to members
2. Members vote on it (supermajority required)
3. If approved, we negotiate publicly (regular updates on the platform)
4. Final deal goes to members for final ratification

Members decide, not the leadership. We're not hiding in backrooms making deals.

Money & Donations

Where does the money come from?
Members donate. That's it.

We have a "Live Treasury" — all donations are public. You can see exactly how much came in and where it went. No dark money, no mysterious donors.

The donation formula has steep diminishing returns. $10 donation gets decent Merit points. $10,000 donation gets proportionally way less. Wealthy people can't buy the movement.
Can corporations donate?
Yes, but it won't get them much influence. The logarithmic formula means large donations have heavily diminished returns.

A corporation donating $10,000 gets roughly the same Merit influence as someone donating $50. We think that's fair.

Plus, all donations are public. If a company donates and then gets favorable policies, everyone can see the correlation.
