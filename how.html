How It Works

A digital ladder to real power. Here's exactly how you climb it.

1

3

Earn Merit — Transparently

Your Merit Score is your influence in the movement. Not based on money, not based on who you know — based on what you actually contribute.

You earn points by voting on proposals, writing successful policies, bringing in active members, completing tasks on the bounty board, and donating (with steep diminishing returns — $10 gets more influence proportionally than $10,000).

Old contributions decay over time (18-month half-life) so you have to keep participating. Sitting on early membership doesn't grant eternal power.

Higher merit opens doors: funding for your proposals, leadership roles, and voting weight on key decisions. The app handles all the math — you just contribute. Read the Whitepaper for full details.

4

Vote — Directly on Laws

No middlemen. No parliament outsourcing. Every member votes directly on laws, budgets, and rules — down to the specific clause level.

  • Your voice counts exactly once — no gerrymandering
  • Laws are living documents you can change when they stop making sense
  • Support, oppose, or abstain — and explain why publicly
  • Right now: one-person-one-vote. Later: merit-weighted (but your vote still counts)
5

Lead — If You Want To

When we form government, any qualified member can apply for any high government post — Cabinet Minister, Ambassador, Independent Commission President, SOE Director, Government SEO Chairperson. Every role treated like a real job application. No family connections, no backroom deals.

The selection process will be nationally televised: live interviews judged by top experts, with public voting. The winner is announced live on TV.

Every appointee goes through a 3-month probation and annual performance reviews — just like any professional employee. Term limits: 2 years max. Recallable by member vote.

🗳️ What Happens When We Win

Our elected candidates are bound to the "People's Manifesto" — the set of policies that members already voted for. If a representative consistently votes against the platform's referendums, we initiate a recall and field a challenger.

It's a digital leash. They're delegates, not rulers.
